Project Overview

Abstract

Quantum computers threaten current encryption (RSA, ECC) via Shor's algorithm and the "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" risk. NIST standardized post-quantum algorithms in 2024, but organizations lack clear governance guidance for transition.

This project introduces PQC-GF, a five-domain framework (QRAS, PQPA, IAD, OAM, CAA) to guide systematic adoption of quantum-resistant cryptography. Supported by 128 public survey responses, pre/post awareness training with 35 students, and four working prototypes.

Core Goals

Objectives

  • Analyze quantum threats to existing cryptographic systems
  • Study NIST post-quantum standards and migration strategies
  • Develop a practical governance framework for organizations
  • Measure awareness through surveys and educational intervention
  • Build & compare classical vs post-quantum cryptographic prototypes
Our Approach

Methodology

Research Method

Design Science Research + mixed methods (surveys + prototypes)

Key Activities

Gap analysis of existing frameworks • 3 surveys • 4 prototypes (Kyber, Dilithium, RSA, BB84)

Tools & Standards

NIST PQC • COBIT 5 • ISO 27001 • Python-based implementations

Key Outcomes

Results

Awareness Survey (128 respondents)

Only 39.1% knew about post-quantum cryptography despite high general cyber awareness

Student Training (35 participants)

Awareness increased from 20% → 45.7% after presentation

Prototypes Performance

Kyber: <20ms • Dilithium: 127–167ms • Practical & efficient
