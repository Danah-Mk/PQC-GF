Project Summary

Abstract

The emergence of quantum computing poses an unexpected threat to current cryptography, as quantum algorithms like Shor's algorithm will make widely used encryption methods such as RSA and ECC obsolete. The “Harvest Now, Decrypt Later” threat introduces another level of urgency. Although NIST standardized post-quantum algorithms in 2024, current IT governance standards lack clear transition requirements.

This research developed the Post-Quantum Cryptography Governance Framework (PQC-GF), a comprehensive roadmap enabling organizations to systematically transition to quantum-resistant cryptographic systems. The PQC-GF consists of five domains: QRAS, PQPA, IAD, OAM, and CAA. Three surveys were conducted: an awareness survey with 128 public respondents, and pre- and post-presentation surveys for 35 cybersecurity students. Four cryptographic prototypes demonstrate the differences between classical cryptography and post-quantum cryptography in practical scenarios. The PQC-GF fills the existing gap by offering an applicable framework that is tailored based on organizational maturity level, risk profile, and availability of resources.

Project Goals

Objectives

  1. Analyze the quantum threat landscape and vulnerabilities in current cryptographic systems.
  2. Evaluate NIST post-quantum standards and their applicability in organizational settings.
  3. Identify gaps in existing IT governance frameworks regarding quantum readiness.
  4. Design and validate a comprehensive governance framework (PQC-GF) for post-quantum transition.
  5. Conduct surveys to measure awareness and assess the impact of educational interventions.
  6. Develop and benchmark prototypes comparing classical and post-quantum cryptographic performance.
Research Approach

Methodology

Research Design

Design Science Research methodology combined with mixed methods: literature review, gap analysis, surveys, and prototype development.

Key Components

Three surveys (128 public + 35 students pre/post), gap analysis of COBIT, ISO 27001, NIST frameworks, and four working prototypes (Kyber, Dilithium, RSA, BB84 QKD).

Standards & Tools

Based on NIST PQC standards, COBIT 5, ISO/IEC 27001. Prototypes implemented using Python-based cryptographic libraries.

Findings & Impact

Results

Awareness Survey (128 respondents)

87.5% aware of general cybersecurity, but only 39.1% familiar with post-quantum cryptography.

Student Surveys (35 participants)

Awareness rose from 20% to 45.7% after educational presentation. 85.7% found it useful/very useful.

Prototype Performance

CRYSTALS-Kyber: <20 ms (text/image encryption)
CRYSTALS-Dilithium: 127–167 ms (signatures)
Practical and efficient for real-world use.
